Why Hello There

When I was a kid (and well into my early teenage years), I had no interest in clothing. My wardrobe consisted of leggings, velcro Sketchers, and rotatable sprit shirts (Proof). I was a creature of habit and hated being uncomfortable. Today, I can’t seem to get enough of fashion. I hope to open up a boutique of my own one-day, but for now I am focusing all my efforts into fashion merchandising, buying, and project management.

Covered in roses

I felt inspired after seeing the collaboration between Free People and For Love & Lemons Skivves in early November. Rather than purchasing one of these bralettes, I decided to make one myself. One creation quickly turned into two. I dabbled with different designs, trying to create something intimate yet loud.

Shawl for fall

I fell in love with this paisley inspired velvet at the craft store. I paired it with some maroon fringe and made it into a shawl.

Thousands of miles away

A few years ago my boyfriend Tyler took a three-month trip to South America. I wanted to make him something that he could wear, reminding himself of me throughout all the distance. I designed and sewed this sweatshirt for him.

Frontier Union

Tyler and I have additionally collaborated to conceptualize and design a mini tee shirt company called Frontier Union. Currently, we are selling our products at Heritage Dry Goods, however we are looking to expand this project.

A modern farm to table

I collaborated with HOLAH Design, Heritage Dry Goods, MADE Studio, and Cedar and Moss to design and furnish a setting that promotes clean and modern design. We strived to provide goods that were locally sourced, highlighting the amazing designers and fabricators that are here in our Pacific North West. Nicole Desch, shopkeeper of Heritage Dry Goods, and I were the stylists for this project.

Heritage Dry Goods

I am currently the intern and assistant buyer for Heritage Dry Goods. This little shop, located in Eugene’s up and coming downtown, is a modern day general store that focuses on American Made products and vintage treasures. I spend most of my days buying merchandise for the store, stylizing displays, updating the store’s online presence, and analyzing budgets.
